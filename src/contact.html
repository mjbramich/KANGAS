Frequently asked questions

Pressure cleaning, also known as pressure washing, utilises a high-pressure stream of water to effectively eliminate dirt, grime, and debris from surfaces such as homes, businesses, decks, driveways, and patios.

The main difference between soft washing and pressure cleaning is the pressure of the water. Pressure cleaning uses a high-pressure stream of water to remove dirt and grime, while soft washing uses a lower pressure with gentle cleaning solutions. Soft washing is less likely to damage your property and is often used on delicate surfaces, such as rendered homes or terracotta roof tiles.

Signs that indicate a need for pressure cleaning include:

  • Dirt, grime, or other debris on the exterior of your property
  • Stains on your deck, driveway, or patio
  • Mould or mildew growth on your property

If you’re unsure whether or not you need pressure cleaning or soft washing, you can always reach out to us for a free estimate!

We understand the importance of your garden’s well-being and take every precaution necessary to prevent harm. Rest assured, we prioritise the safety of your garden and ensure its protection during our cleaning process.

We are based in Latrobe, Tasmania and we service the whole North-West coast of Tasmania.

Our high-pressure washing system can effectively clean a wide range of surfaces, including fascia, eaves, roofs, weatherboards, pavers, terracotta tiles, render, colorbond, cladding, cement, slate, sandstone, and more.

Our pressure cleaning prices vary based on factors such as the area’s size, height, and degree of dirtiness. For an obligation-free quote, please contact us, and we will find a solution that best suits your needs.
